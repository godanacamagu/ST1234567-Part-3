1.1 Brief History 
Golden Palm Hotel was established in 2008 in Durban, KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa. Founded by 
the Naidoo family with a vision to bring warm African hospitality and modern luxury together, the 
hotel began as a 24-room boutique property on the Durban beachfront. Over the years, it expanded 
to 72 elegantly appointed rooms and suites, gaining a reputation as one of the city's premier coastal 
destinations. 
By 2015, Golden Palm Hotel had earned multiple regional hospitality awards, including Best 
Boutique Hotel in KwaZulu-Natal. The hotel has undergone two major refurbishments — in 2013 
and 2020 — to modernise its facilities while preserving its distinctly warm, African-influenced design 
aesthetic. It is a proud member of the South African Tourism and Hospitality Association (SATHA). 
1.2 Mission Statement 
"To deliver an unparalleled guest experience through personalised service, luxurious comfort, 
and authentic African hospitality that creates lasting memories for every visitor." 
1.3 Vision Statement 
"To be the most celebrated boutique hotel destination on the South African coastline, 
recognised globally for our commitment to excellence, sustainability, and cultural authenticity."

3. Current Website Analysis 
Golden Palm Hotel currently operates a basic informational website created in 2019. The following 
analysis has been conducted to inform the redesign process. 
3.1 Strengths of the Existing Website 
• Contains basic contact information and physical address 
• Includes a photo gallery with some high-quality room imagery 
• Lists room types and general pricing tiers 
• Has a functioning contact form for enquiries

3.2 Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement 
• No integrated online booking engine — all reservations directed to third-party platforms 
• Poor mobile responsiveness; the site is not optimised for smartphones or tablets 
• Outdated visual design that does not reflect the hotel's current luxury branding 
• Slow page load times due to uncompressed images and legacy code 
• No search engine optimisation (SEO) strategy in place 
• Absence of a blog or content marketing section to drive organic traffic 
• No live chat or WhatsApp integration for customer support 
• Missing social media feeds and review integration (e.g., TripAdvisor, Google Reviews) 
• No event/conference enquiry section with detailed venue specifications 
4. Proposed Website Features and Functionality 
4.1 Core Pages 
• Homepage — Hero banner, key value propositions, featured rooms, testimonials, and CTAs 
• About Us — Hotel history, team profiles, awards, and certifications 
• Rooms & Suites — Individual room pages with photo galleries, amenities, and pricing 
• Dining — Restaurant menus, hours, atmosphere gallery, and reservation links 
• Facilities & Amenities — Spa, pool, gym, and beachfront descriptions with imagery 
• Events & Conferences — Venue floor plans, capacity tables, and enquiry form 
• Weddings — Package details, photo gallery, and booking enquiry form 
• Gallery — High-resolution, categorised image and video gallery 
• Special Offers — Dynamic promotions and seasonal packages 
• Blog / News — Travel tips, local attractions, and hotel news for SEO 
• Contact Us — Map integration, contact form, WhatsApp button, and live chat 
4.2 Key Functionality 
• Online Booking Engine — Real-time room availability calendar with secure payment 
gateway (PayFast / PayGate) 
• Mobile-First Responsive Design — Fully optimised across all device types 
• SEO Optimisation — Structured data, meta tags, sitemap, and Google Analytics integration 
• Social Media Integration — Instagram feed, Facebook page linking, sharing buttons 
• Review Aggregator — TripAdvisor and Google Reviews widget 
• WhatsApp Chat Button — Direct messaging for instant guest support 
• Newsletter Subscription — Email capture with Mailchimp integration 
• Multi-Currency Display — ZAR as primary, with USD/GBP/EUR conversion indicators

5.4 Layout and Design Approach 
• Full-screen hero video or high-quality photograph on the homepage with an overlay booking 
widget 
• Grid-based room display with hover effects revealing key room details and a direct 'Book 
Now' CTA 
• Sticky navigation bar that transitions from transparent to solid-gold on scroll 
• Generous whitespace to create a premium, uncluttered feel 
• Asymmetric editorial layouts in the About and Dining sections for visual interest 
• Full-bleed image sections alternating with text panels for visual rhythm

5.5 User Experience Considerations 
• Navigation — Maximum three-click depth to any page; primary navigation with clear 
dropdown menus 
• Accessibility — WCAG 2.1 AA compliant; adequate colour contrast, alt text for all images, 
keyboard navigation 
• Booking Flow — Streamlined 3-step booking process: Select Dates → Choose Room → 
Confirm & Pay 
• Page Load Speed — Target under 2.5 seconds; lazy image loading, CDN implementation 
• Trust Signals — Prominently displayed star ratings, security badges, and guest testimonials
